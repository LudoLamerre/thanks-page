Your First Tandem Skydive: 10 Clear Steps

A tandem skydive is the easiest way to experience freefall. You’re securely attached to a professional instructor who handles the gear and procedures while you focus on the thrill and the views over the Pyrenees.

  1. Book your jump with Azimut Tandem.
  2. Arrive on time; bring ID and follow briefing instructions.
  3. Gear up: harness fitting + safety checks.
  4. Climb to altitude (typically 3,000–4,000 m).
  5. Door open: position, smile, breathe.
  6. Freefall: ~35–50 seconds of pure adrenaline.
  7. Parachute opens: 5–7 minutes of scenic flight.
  8. Gentle landing with instructor guidance.
  9. Debrief + optional video/photos review.
  10. Celebrate — you did it!

Safety & Requirements in France (Occitanie)

Tandem skydives are performed under strict regulations and by certified instructors. Typical requirements include a minimum age (often 15–16 with consent), adherence to weight and health guidelines, and a mandatory safety briefing.

  • Briefing first: Procedures, body position, and landing explained clearly.
  • Equipment: Professionally maintained tandem parachute systems with reserve and AAD.
  • Weather: Jumps proceed only in safe conditions (visibility, wind, cloud base).

Always follow your instructor’s guidance. If you have medical concerns, consult your doctor before booking.

Best Season & Weather Tips in the Pyrenees

The Pyrenees offer spectacular mountain views. Spring to early autumn often brings the most stable conditions, but great jump days can happen year-round. Flexibility helps when weather changes.

  • Plan ahead: Book early, choose a date with backup options.
  • Be flexible: Wind and cloud cover may shift schedules.
  • Layers: Temperatures are cooler at altitude even in summer.

Tandem vs. AFF: Which One Is Right for You?

Tandem is perfect for a first jump: minimal training, maximum experience. AFF (Accelerated Freefall) is a training path that leads to solo skydiving licenses and requires more time and commitment.

  • Tandem: Instructor controls the skydive; you enjoy the ride.
  • AFF: You learn skills to deploy and fly your own canopy.
  • Tip: Try tandem first; if you love it, consider an AFF course later.

What to Wear & What to Bring

Dress for comfort and safety. Avoid loose scarves or jewelry. Tie long hair and wear secure shoes (no open-toed footwear). Bring a valid ID and arrive hydrated and rested.

  • Clothes: Comfortable layers; avoid bulky hoods.
  • Shoes: Lace-up sneakers are ideal.
  • Extras: Water, sunscreen, and a light snack.
